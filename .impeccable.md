## Design Context

### Users

Everyday calorie trackers who want to log a meal quickly, usually on a phone immediately after eating or shopping. They need a trustworthy daily overview, clear portions, a simple target setup, and optional cross-device continuity without being forced through a complex nutrition program.

### Brand Personality

Calm, candid, and capable. Calorie Flow should feel like a considered pocket notebook: encouraging without being performatively cheerful, precise without becoming clinical, and private by default.

### Aesthetic Direction

Use a dark, low-glare "night-kitchen ledger" direction. The interface should feel composed in a dim kitchen or after a workout: deep botanical neutrals, restrained herb-green emphasis, food-forward imagery where available, and a compact desktop workspace rather than a stretched mobile layout. Avoid generic wellness gradients, dashboard clutter, and decorative data visualisations.

### Design Principles

1. One obvious next action: logging food always outranks secondary features.
2. The daily total is a calm orientation tool, never a judgement.
3. Complexity stays available but hidden until a user asks for it.
4. Local privacy and account choice are plainly explained at the moment they matter.
5. Every screen must be legible and comfortable on a one-handed phone as well as a wide desktop display.
